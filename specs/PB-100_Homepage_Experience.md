# Design Philosophy

The homepage is not a dashboard.

The homepage is not the City website.

The homepage is not a campaign website.

The homepage is an invitation.

Its purpose is to connect residents with the people, projects, and conversations shaping Collegedale.

Visitors should leave the homepage wanting to explore further.

---

# Homepage Goals

Within ten seconds a visitor should understand:

• This website is about Collegedale.

• Laura is actively serving the community.

• There are meaningful ways to stay informed and participate.

# Mobile Philosophy

The homepage should feel like an app.

Each major section should fit comfortably within one phone screen.

Large touch targets.

Large typography.

Minimal scrolling within sections.

Fast loading.

---

# Relationship to the City's Website

Project Beacon supplements the official City website.

Official resources such as agendas, ordinances, departments, forms, and services should link directly to the City's website rather than duplicate information.

Project Beacon focuses on:

Explaining

Connecting

Documenting

Engaging

Celebrating

The City website focuses on:

Government services

Official records

Departments

Permits

Utilities

Administration

The two websites should complement one another rather than compete.

# Success

Residents return regularly because they trust Project Beacon as the best place to understand what is happening in their community.

Laura's work is demonstrated naturally through transparency, communication, and documented public service rather than promotional language.

Every page reinforces one central idea:

Working Together for Collegedale.
