# Project Beacon

## Project Vision

**Version:** 1.1 (Draft)

**Last Updated:** June 2026

---

# Executive Summary

Project Beacon is a civic engagement platform designed to strengthen communication between Commissioner Laura Howse and the residents of Collegedale, Tennessee.

The platform serves two complementary purposes:

1. Provide residents with a trusted, easy-to-understand source of information about the City of Collegedale.
2. Create a transparent, evidence-based record of Commissioner Laura Howse's service to the community.

Rather than functioning as a traditional political website, Project Beacon demonstrates Laura's work through official public records, measurable outcomes, community involvement, and ongoing communication.

Residents should be able to evaluate Laura's service based on documented facts rather than campaign messaging.

---

# Mission Statement

**Build the most trusted online source for understanding what's happening in Collegedale while providing a transparent record of Commissioner Laura Howse's service to the community.**

---

# Vision

We believe local government works best when residents have easy access to accurate information, understand why decisions are made, and feel invited to participate in shaping the future of their community.

Project Beacon exists to reduce the distance between city government and the people it serves.

The platform should celebrate the residents, businesses, schools, churches, volunteers, and organizations that make Collegedale exceptional while documenting the work that goes into responsible public service.

---

# Purpose

Project Beacon has five primary purposes.

## 1. Inform

Provide timely, accurate, and easy-to-understand information about:

* Commission meetings
* Community CARE meetings
* City projects
* Community events
* Economic development
* Public safety
* Parks and recreation
* Planning initiatives

---

## 2. Connect

Encourage meaningful communication between Commissioner Laura Howse and the residents of Collegedale.

The website should make it easy for residents to:

* Learn
* Ask questions
* Participate
* Stay informed

---

## 3. Demonstrate

Provide a transparent, evidence-based record of Laura's work as a City Commissioner.

Rather than relying on general statements or campaign-style messaging, the platform documents Laura's service through official public records and measurable activities, including:

* Commission meeting attendance
* Voting record
* Agenda preparation and project involvement
* Community CARE meetings
* Community events
* Public communications
* Project leadership
* Annual summaries
* Long-term initiatives

Residents should be able to understand not only how Laura voted, but what issues she worked on, how projects evolved, and how she remained engaged throughout her term.

---

## 4. Preserve

Create a permanent digital archive of Collegedale's progress.

The archive should preserve:

* Commission decisions
* Individual votes
* Project timelines
* Official documents
* Community CARE meetings
* Community events
* Historical photography
* Public presentations
* Community milestones

Project Beacon should become an institutional memory for the City, documenting not only what happened, but when, why, and how decisions shaped the community.

---

## 5. Inspire

Celebrate the people who make Collegedale exceptional.

Highlight:

* Students
* Teachers
* Veterans
* Volunteers
* Businesses
* Churches
* Civic organizations
* Public safety personnel
* City employees
* Community leaders

The website should remind residents that strong communities are built through collaboration.

# Guiding Principles

## Community Before Politics

Every page should provide value to residents.

The platform exists to serve the community first.

---

## Evidence Before Opinion

Major statements about Laura's work should be supported by official records whenever possible.

Examples include:

* Meeting minutes
* Voting records
* Agendas
* Project timelines
* Videos
* Public documents
* Community events

---

## Transparency Builds Trust

Residents should always be able to answer:

* What happened?
* Why did it happen?
* How did each commissioner vote?
* What happens next?

---

## Explain Government Clearly

Translate complex government processes into language that residents can easily understand while maintaining links to the official record.

---

## Build Once. Reuse Everywhere.

Information entered once should automatically support:

* Website pages
* Project timelines
* Meeting summaries
* CARE meetings
* Newsletters
* Annual reports
* Social media

---

## Preserve the Story

Today's commission meeting becomes tomorrow's history.

Every project, decision, vote, photograph, and community event contributes to the continuing story of Collegedale.

# Success Measures

Project Beacon will be successful when:

* Residents view it as the primary source for understanding city government.
* Laura spends less time creating updates because information is organized and reusable.
* Community CARE meetings become a recognized source of civic engagement.
* Every major city project has a complete, searchable history.
* Residents can easily understand commission decisions and voting records.
* The platform becomes a trusted archive of Laura's service and Collegedale's continued growth.

# Product Philosophy

Project Beacon should feel less like a political website and more like a trusted civic publication.

Visitors should leave with a better understanding of:

* Their city
* Their local government
* Their community
* The work being performed on their behalf

Every design decision should answer one question:

> **Does this help residents better understand Collegedale and the work being done to serve it?**

---

# Closing Statement

Technology does not build stronger communities.

Communication, transparency, preparation, and consistent public service do.

Project Beacon exists to document those efforts, strengthen civic engagement, preserve the history of Collegedale, and provide residents with a trusted resource that demonstrates the value of informed, thoughtful local leadership.
